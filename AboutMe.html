About Me

Who am I?

My name is Danielle Hall. I grew up in the United States (due to my American father) and moved to Norway when I was 14 (due to my Norwegian-Filipino mother). Because of my interesting upbringing and cultural background, I have developed into someone who thrives when met with challenges and adversity. Learning Data Science began as a simple interest that I would delve into in my free time, but I quickly decided I wanted a career in this fascinating, ever-evolving, and challenging field.

What is my Education?

My formal education consists of one finished degree and one degree that I am currently undertaking:

Bachelor's of Science in Psychology from the University of Essex

While undertaking this degree, I developed great interpersonal communication skills and a deep understanding of human behavior. This is also where my love for data began, as I delved into statistics, research, and due to my thesis, (How the design of maps affected their usability) learned about usability research and user experience design.

Master's of Science in Data Science from the University of Essex

While undertaking this degree, I hope to develop the skills necessary to become a data scientist, further my understanding of the responsibilities and role of a data scientist, and use my unique perspective as a student with a psychology background to create more constructive collaborations and discussions with my peers.

IBM Data Science Professional Certificate

This course - offered by the International Business Machines Corporation - has given me the foundation to call myself a data scientist, with skills such as python, SQL, data visualization, machine learning, and more.

What am I doing Currently?

With my background as a psychology graduate, I began working as a subsitute in a psychiatric ward. I started out working during the day, using environmental therapy techniques to treat the patients of the ward. When I got more experience, I was offered a longer contract as an official worker and not just an on-call subsitute - with the condition that I would only be working night shifts. That leads me to where I am today, coding this portfolio and doing school work quietly while the patients are asleep in their rooms.

In my free time, I can be found consuming concerningly large amounts of literature, playing online first person shooters, and fervently researching whatever topic I've most recently gotten obsessed with.
